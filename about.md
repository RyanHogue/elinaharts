# How We Review

We're a fashion-research site, not a testing lab — and we think that's a strength, not a weakness,
as long as we're honest about it. This page explains exactly how our guides are built, what we can
tell you, and what we can't.

---

## What we actually do

For every guide we:

1. **Pull the brand's live product data** — every price, size, fabric weight and fibre content,
   read directly from the store's own catalog on the date shown at the top of the article.
2. **Convert everything to one unit** so brands can be compared fairly (for example, fabric weight
   in ounces converted to GSM at 33.906 g/m² per oz/yd²).
3. **Summarize what verified buyers report** — recurring themes across published customer reviews,
   not a single cherry-picked quote.
4. **Score each option** on a transparent, weighted system (below), and show the working.

Everything is dated, and everything is sourced.

---

## Our scoring system

Every product guide uses the same weighted criteria, so you can see *why* one option ranked above
another rather than taking our word for it:

| Criterion | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Fabric substance** | 30% | Published GSM / oz weight and fibre content — the most reliable printed signal of how a garment hangs and lasts. |
| **Fit & size range** | 25% | How many sizes exist and how far the range extends, counted style by style. |
| **Range depth** | 20% | Number of live styles and in-stock variants. |
| **Cultural authenticity** | 15% | Ownership and heritage, verified through independent editorial coverage. |
| **Value** | 10% | Price measured against fabric weight — not price alone. |

## What we can — and can't — tell you

**We can** verify, from a distance: price, fabric weight, fibre content, size availability, and the
brand's own published fit guidance. These are printed facts, and we check them against the source.

**We can't** tell you how a garment feels after twenty washes, whether the stitching holds, or how
true a colour looks in daylight — because **no one here has physically handled the item.** No spec
sheet answers those questions, and we never pretend otherwise. Where long-term durability matters
most to you, buy one piece before committing to a wardrobe.

You'll never read the words "I tested this" or "I wore it for months" on this site. We didn't, and
saying so would be a lie.

---

## Why trust research over a single reviewer

One person can wear a hoodie and tell you how it felt on *them*. What they usually can't do is read
every brand's catalog, convert incompatible fabric-weight units, count size availability across
hundreds of styles, and line it all up in one table. That's the gap we fill — the comparison work
that's tedious for a human but is exactly where the buying decision actually lives.

## How this site makes money

Some links on our guides are **affiliate links**. If you buy through one, we may earn a commission
at no extra cost to you. That's it — brands don't pay us for placement or for a good ranking, and a
product can't buy its way up our scoring. If it could, the scoring would be worthless, and so would
we.

Every page with affiliate links says so, at the top, before the content.
