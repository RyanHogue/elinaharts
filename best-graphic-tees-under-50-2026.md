## The one number that separates them

Graphic tees live in three weight bands, and price does **not** reliably tell you which you're getting:

| Weight | What it feels like |
|---|---|
| **130–160 GSM** (≈4–4.7 oz) | Light summer tee. Thin, drapes close, can go slightly sheer in pale colours. |
| **180–220 GSM** (≈5.3–6.5 oz) | Standard tee. What most printed shirts use. |
| **250–360 GSM** (≈7.4–10.6 oz) | Heavyweight / boxy tee. Holds its shape, doesn't cling, feels substantial. |

The surprise in this price range: the **heaviest** tee we found under $50 (360 GSM) is also one of
the **cheaper** ones ($33.94), while several $25 tees are the lightest (142 GSM). Weight and price
aren't the same axis — so shop on both.

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the best graphic tee under $50?**
For fabric, the Jamroc Heavyweight Mineral Wash Boxy Tee — 360 GSM for $33.94, the heaviest we found
in this range. For design choice, Farm Brand (53 styles from $24) or Cooyah (68 styles from $14).

**Why are some $25 tees thinner than a $34 one?**
Because weight and price aren't the same thing. A $25 tee is often 142 GSM (light); the $34 Jamroc
boxy tee is 360 GSM (heavy). Always check the GSM, not just the price.

**What GSM should a good graphic tee be?**
180–220 GSM is a solid everyday standard. Below ~150 GSM it's a light summer tee; 250 GSM and up is
a heavyweight, boxy feel.

**How do I convert oz to GSM?**
Multiply by 33.9. A 4.2 oz tee is about 142 GSM; a 6 oz tee is about 203 GSM.

**Are these graphics original designs?**
The picks here are the brands' own designs. We deliberately left out shirts using third-party
trademarks (band names, logos, characters) — those carry legal risk for the buyer and seller alike.

**Do they ship to the US?**
All three run US direct-to-consumer storefronts. Confirm shipping at checkout.
