"Heavyweight" is the most abused word in hoodies. Brands slap it on 300 GSM fleece and 500 GSM
cotton alike, and the product photo looks identical either way. The only thing that actually tells
you what you're getting is **GSM** — grams per square metre, the printed weight of the fabric — and
most brands never publish it.

We pulled the live catalogs of three apparel brands and ranked their hoodies by the weights they
*do* publish, from 271 all the way to 530 GSM. Here's what's genuinely heavyweight, what only claims
to be, and which one is worth its price.

## First: what "heavyweight" actually means

One conversion unlocks the whole category. **1 oz/yd² ≈ 33.9 GSM**, so a hoodie sold as "8 oz" is
about 271 GSM. Here's how the trade reads the numbers:

| Weight | What it is |
|---|---|
| 280–350 GSM (8.3–10.3 oz) | **Standard** hoodie. Most "hoodies" live here. |
| 350–400 GSM | Solid mid-heavy. Noticeably substantial. |
| **400 GSM+ (11.8 oz+)** | **Genuine heavyweight.** Structured, warm, heavy in the hand. |
| 500 GSM+ (14.8 oz+) | Outerwear-adjacent. Holds its shape rather than draping. |

By that standard, a lot of "heavyweight" hoodies — including most of Farm Brand's cotton range at
271–281 GSM — are really **standard weight**. The genuine heavyweights below start at 400 GSM.

## Who should NOT buy a heavyweight hoodie

- ❌ **You live somewhere warm.** 500+ GSM cotton will cook you. A 280–350 GSM hoodie is the
  everyday range; save the heavyweight for real cold.
- ❌ **You want it light for layering.** Heavyweight hoodies are bulky under a jacket. That's the
  point, and the problem, depending on your use.
- ❌ **You're shopping purely on the GSM number.** A 417 GSM polyester-sherpa hoodie and a 417 GSM
  cotton hoodie are different garments. Read the fibre.
- ❌ **You need a specific style above 2XL.** The heaviest cotton hoodies here top out at XL–2XL.
- ❌ **You want cheap.** Genuine 500 GSM cotton starts around $110–165. The $40 options are standard
  weight, honestly labelled.

## Frequently asked questions

**What GSM counts as a "heavyweight" hoodie?**
Roughly **400 GSM and up**. Standard hoodies are 280–350 GSM. At 400+ the fabric feels structured
and heavy; at 500+ it behaves closer to outerwear.

**What's the heaviest hoodie here?**
Jamroc's 530 GSM 100% cotton hoodies (Resilience and the zip version). That's about double a standard
hoodie's weight.

**Is Farm Brand's 417 GSM hoodie heavier than Jamroc's 410 GSM one?**
On paper, barely — but they're not comparable. Farm Brand's 417 is a **polyester sherpa lining**;
Jamroc's 410 is **cotton**. The Farm Brand is warmer; the Jamroc feels denser and more structured.

**How do I convert oz to GSM?**
Multiply ounces by 33.9. An 8 oz hoodie is about 271 GSM; a 12 oz one is about 407 GSM.

**Is a heavier hoodie always better quality?**
No. Weight tells you density and warmth, not stitching or durability. It's a strong signal of
substance, not a guarantee of quality — and we haven't handled these to judge the rest.

**Which is the best value?**
On price-per-gram, Farm Brand ($14/100 GSM). On genuine heavyweight *cotton*, Jamroc's $110 Culture
Club Zip is the cheapest way in.

**Do these ship to the US?**
All three run direct-to-consumer US storefronts. Confirm shipping at checkout before ordering.
