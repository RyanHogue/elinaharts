Search "Jamaican streetwear" and you get two kinds of results: magazine features on runway
designers most people will never buy, and dropshipping stores selling the same green-gold-black
graphic on a 150 GSM blank.

This comparison sits in between. We pulled the **live product catalogs of six Caribbean-rooted
labels** — **860 products in total** — and lined up the three things that actually decide whether
you'll wear the piece: **what it costs, how heavy the fabric is, and whether it comes in your size.**

Two findings surprised us enough to lead with them.

**The size ranges are wildly different.** One brand here carries 15 styles up to 6XL. Three others
stop at 2XL across their entire catalogs. If you wear above a 2XL, that single fact eliminates most
of this category before price enters the conversation.

**Only one brand publishes fabric weight at all.** Every other label expects you to judge cloth from
a photograph.

## How we scored these brands

We do not have these garments in hand. Rather than pretend otherwise, we built the comparison from
things that can be verified from a distance and checked against the brands' own live data.

**Every price, size count and fabric weight below was read directly from each brand's public product
feed on July 23, 2026.** Nothing here is recalled from memory or copied from another roundup.

Our weighting:

| Criterion | Weight | What we measured |
|---|---|---|
| **Fabric substance** | 30% | Published GSM (grams per square metre) and fibre content. Weight is the most reliable printed indicator of how a garment hangs and lasts. |
| **Fit & size range** | 25% | How many sizes exist, and how far the range actually extends — counted style by style, not taken from marketing claims. |
| **Range depth** | 20% | Number of live styles and in-stock variants. A shallow catalog means your size sells out and never returns. |
| **Cultural authenticity** | 15% | Jamaican or Caribbean ownership and heritage, verified through independent editorial coverage rather than the brand's own copy. |
| **Value** | 10% | Price measured against fabric weight — not price alone. |

**What we could not assess:** how a garment feels after twenty washes, whether stitching holds, and
how true the colours look in daylight. No published spec answers those. Where long-term durability
matters most to you, buy one piece before committing to a wardrobe.

## What to look for when you can't touch the fabric

If you take one thing from this page, make it this: **GSM tells you more than any product photo.**
Grams per square metre is the printed weight of the cloth, and it maps directly onto how a garment
behaves.

As a general guide to how the apparel trade uses these numbers:

| Weight | What it usually means |
|---|---|
| **130–180 GSM** | Light summer tee. Thin, drapes close, often semi-sheer in pale colours. |
| **180–220 GSM** | Standard tee weight. What most printed shirts use. |
| **220–280 GSM** | Heavyweight tee. Holds its shape, boxier, noticeably more substantial. |
| **280–350 GSM** | Standard hoodie or sweatshirt. |
| **400 GSM+** | Heavyweight hoodie. Structured, warm, heavy in the hand. |
| **500 GSM+** | Outerwear-adjacent. Very dense; holds shape rather than draping. |

Applied here: Jamroc's 430 GSM tee sits at roughly **double** a standard printed tee, and its 530 GSM
hoodie is above the usual heavyweight band entirely.

**Three more checks before buying in this category:**

1. **Open the size dropdown on the exact product**, not the brand's size chart. Extended sizes are
   almost always offered on a subset of styles.
2. **Read the fibre content, not just the weight.** A heavy cotton-polyester blend behaves very
   differently from heavy 100% cotton.
3. **Check the return policy before ordering across borders.** Return shipping is where a good deal
   quietly becomes a bad one.

## Who should NOT buy from these brands

Being direct saves you money:

- ❌ **You need a specific piece above 4XL.** Only Jamroc goes further, and only on 24 styles at 5XL
  and 15 at 6XL. If your size and your preferred design don't overlap, walk away rather than
  size-guessing.
- ❌ **You want cheap blank basics.** The entry point here is a $14 Cooyah graphic tee. For unbranded
  blanks, this whole category is the wrong aisle.
- ❌ **You're buying a gift and don't know their size.** Fit here spans fitted crops through oversized
  530 GSM hoodies. Without a size, a gift card is the honest answer.
- ❌ **You need it this week.** Several of these are small labels; dispatch times vary and are not
  Amazon-fast.
- ❌ **You refuse to buy without printed fabric specs.** Only one brand here publishes GSM. That makes
  your shortlist one brand long.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which Jamaican streetwear brand has the widest size range?**
Jamroc Dept., by a wide margin — 2XS through 6XL, with 56 styles at 4XL, 24 at 5XL and 15 at 6XL.
Cooyah reaches 5XL on a single style. Theophilio tops out at 3XL on one style, and both British
labels stop at 2XL.

**Is Caribbean streetwear expensive?**
It splits into two tiers. Cooyah and Jamroc Dept. sell everyday pieces from roughly $14 to $225.
Theophilio, Bianca Saunders and Nicholas Daley operate as designer labels reaching $385–$1,250.

**What does GSM mean on a hoodie?**
Grams per square metre — the fabric's weight. Standard hoodies run 280–350 GSM. At 400 GSM and above
a hoodie feels structured and heavy; at 530 GSM it behaves closer to outerwear.

**Which brand is best for graphic t-shirts?**
Cooyah, on both selection and price: 105 tee styles from $14. Jamroc Dept. offers 61 tee styles at
$20–120, with published fabric weights on many of them.

**Are these brands actually Jamaican-owned?**
Heritage varies and the distinction matters. Jamroc Dept., Cooyah and Tribe Nine Studios are
Jamaica-rooted. Theophilio was founded by a Jamaican-American designer and is based in Brooklyn.
Bianca Saunders and Nicholas Daley are British-Jamaican labels based in the UK.

**Do these brands ship to the US?**
Theophilio is carried by SSENSE with US shipping, and the others run direct-to-consumer storefronts.
Shipping terms change — confirm at the brand's own checkout before ordering.

**Is a heavier hoodie always better?**
No. A 530 GSM hoodie is warm, structured and holds its shape, but it is heavy, slow to dry and too
much for a mild climate. For layering in warm weather, 280–350 GSM is more practical.

**Why aren't there more brands on this list?**
Because we only included labels whose live catalogs we could verify. Several frequently-cited names —
including Trendzy JA and TVFF — appear in editorial coverage but did not expose a public product
catalog we could check prices and sizes against. We would rather publish six verified brands than
twelve half-checked ones.

## Methodology and limitations

Product counts, prices, size availability and fabric weights were read from each brand's public
product feed on **July 23, 2026**, covering **860 products** in total. Where a catalog exceeded one
page of results, we pulled every page — an earlier draft of this comparison undercounted two brands
by stopping at the first page, which is exactly the error that makes most roundups unreliable.

Brand backgrounds were checked against the independent editorial coverage listed below rather than
each brand's own marketing.

**We have not physically handled any of these garments.** Everything above comes from published
specifications, catalog data and independent reporting. Fabric weight, size availability and price
can be verified from a distance; feel, durability and true colour cannot — and we have not claimed
otherwise anywhere on this page.

Prices and stock change constantly. Check the current listing before buying.
