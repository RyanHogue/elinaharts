## Short answer

**No. Farm Brand does not run small — it's designed true to size.**

We read the product data for all **186 live Farm Brand products**, and the brand makes **no
"runs small" claim anywhere**. What it does say is the opposite of a warning: on 48 products it adds
one optional note — *"We'd recommend you order one size up from your normal size, if you prefer a
looser fit."* That's advice for people who **want** extra room, not a fix for a small cut.

So order your usual size. The only real complication isn't the fit — it's this:

⚠️ **Farm Brand publishes no measurement charts.** Zero of its 186 products list a chest or length
measurement. That's the actual challenge here, and the rest of this guide is how to size correctly
without one.

**The 10-second version:**

| Situation | What to do |
|---|---|
| You want a normal fit | **Order your usual size.** True to size. |
| You want a loose / relaxed fit | **Order one size up** (the brand says so on 48 items). |
| You're between two sizes | Size up — there's no chart to split the difference, and the fabric is mostly light. |
| You wear above 2XL | Check first: only 14 of 186 styles reach 3XL, and nothing goes higher. |

## What the brand actually says about fit

Across the catalog, the sizing guidance is remarkably consistent — because it's literally the same
sentence, repeated on **48 of 186 products**, word for word:

> *"We'd recommend you to order one size up from your normal size, if you prefer to have a looser fit."*

Two things to take from that:

1. **The default is your normal size.** The size-up is conditional — *"if you prefer a looser fit."*
   If you like a standard fit, ignore it.
2. **There is no "it runs small" language, and no "runs large" language, anywhere.** Nor any
   "true to size" label. The brand simply expects you to order normally.

The other 138 products carry no sizing note at all. That puts more weight on knowing how to judge
fit yourself — which brings us to the real issue.

## The real catch: no size charts

This is where Farm Brand differs sharply from a brand like Jamroc Dept., which publishes full
inch-and-centimetre measurement tables on 154 of its products. Farm Brand publishes **none** — not a
single chest, length, or width measurement across 186 items.

That's not a dealbreaker, but it changes how you should buy. Here's how to size accurately anyway:

**1. Measure a garment you already own and like.**
Lay a hoodie or tee you love flat, and measure it: chest (armpit to armpit), and length (collar to
hem). When your Farm Brand order arrives, you can't compare in advance — so instead, buy **one**
piece first, measure it, and use that as your reference for future orders from the same brand.

**2. Let the fabric weight tell you how it will drape.**
Farm Brand *does* publish something more useful than most: fabric weight, in ounces, on 97 of its
products (and fibre content on 184 of 186). Weight predicts behaviour:

| Weight | How it fits and feels |
|---|---|
| ~4.2 oz (≈142 GSM) — most tees | Light, drapes close to the body, moves easily. Not boxy. |
| ~6–7 oz (≈205–240 GSM) — heavier tees, light hoodies | More structured, holds shape a little. |
| ~8 oz (≈270 GSM) — standard hoodies | Substantial, sits away from the body. |
| ~12 oz (≈417 GSM) — sherpa-lined zip hoodie | Thick and structured; wears a touch larger. |

A light 4.2 oz tee will feel closer and softer than the number suggests; a heavier fleece hoodie
will feel roomier. (For the full oz-to-GSM breakdown, see our
[Jamroc vs Farm Brand comparison]({{ '/jamroc-vs-farm-brand-2026' | relative_url }}).)

**3. Read the fit word in the description.**

## Fit vocabulary, decoded

These words appear across Farm Brand's catalog, and each one changes what size to order:

| Term | Appears on | What it means for sizing |
|---|---|---|
| **Unisex** | 24 products | Cut on a straight, men's-style block. Women often size down one. |
| **Slim fit** | 12 products | Closer to the body. Order your normal size, or size up for room. |
| **Relaxed fit** | 12 products | Already roomy. Order your normal size; don't add the size-up. |
| **Fitted** | 11 products | Meant to sit close. This is where the "size up if you want it looser" note matters most. |
| **Oversized** | 7 products | Deliberately large. Order normal for the intended look. |

**The most useful pairing:** a *fitted* or *unisex* piece plus the *"size up for a looser fit"* note
means most people who dislike a close fit should size up **one**. On a *relaxed* or *oversized*
piece, stay at your normal size — sizing up there gets baggy fast.

## Who should NOT order without extra care

- ❌ **You wear above 2XL and want a specific style.** Only 14 styles reach 3XL; none go beyond.
- ❌ **You need a guaranteed exact fit with no returns.** With no size chart, first orders carry more
  risk. Buy one piece, measure it, then reorder confidently.
- ❌ **You want a heavyweight, structured tee.** Most Farm Brand tees are 4.2 oz (≈142 GSM) — light
  and drapey by design. If you want a boxy, substantial tee, this isn't the brand.
- ❌ **You're between sizes on a "relaxed" or "oversized" style.** Sizing up on an already-roomy cut
  gets baggy. Stay at your normal size on those.
- ❌ **You're buying a gift and don't know their fit preference.** The "size up if you like it looser"
  logic depends entirely on how the person likes clothes to sit.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does Farm Brand run small?**
No. It's designed true to size and makes no "runs small" claim. Order your usual size. On 48 products
it suggests sizing up *only if you prefer a looser fit* — that's optional, not a correction.

**Should I size up in a Farm Brand hoodie?**
Only if you want it roomy, or if the description calls it "fitted" or "slim." For a relaxed or
oversized style, stay at your normal size.

**Does Farm Brand have a size chart?**
No — none of its 186 products publish measurement tables. It does publish fabric weight (97 products)
and fibre content (184 of 186), which help predict drape. The safest approach is to buy one piece,
measure it flat, and use that as your reference.

**What's the largest size Farm Brand offers?**
3XL, on just 14 styles. The standard range is XS–2XL, offered on almost the entire catalog. Nothing
goes above 3XL.

**Are Farm Brand's clothes true to size for women?**
On unisex styles (24 of them), the cut is a straight men's-style block, so many women size down one.
Women's-specific and fitted styles run closer to expected.

**Why are the t-shirts so thin?**
Most are 4.2 oz (≈142 GSM) — a light summer-tee weight by design. That's not a defect; it's the
category. If you want a heavier tee, look elsewhere.

**Is Farm Brand actually made in the USA?**
Despite the "USA" in the name, only 3 of 186 products state "Made in USA." Don't assume country of
origin from the brand name — check the individual product page.

**How does Farm Brand's sizing compare to Jamroc Dept.?**
