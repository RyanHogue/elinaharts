## What $165 actually buys

Jamroc's flagship hoodie is **530 GSM, 100% cotton, for $165.** To know if that's fair, you need one
reference point: **a standard hoodie is 280–350 GSM.** Jamroc's is roughly **double the fabric.**

Put it in cost-per-gram terms:

| Hoodie | Weight | Price | $ per 100 GSM |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jamroc 530 GSM cotton | 530 GSM | $165 | **$31** |
| Jamroc 400 GSM cotton | 400 GSM | $135 | $34 |
| Jamroc Culture Club Zip | 410 GSM | $110 | **$27** |
| A typical $50 / 300 GSM hoodie | 300 GSM | ~$50 | ~$17 |

So on pure fabric-per-dollar, Jamroc is **more expensive per gram** than a basic hoodie — you're
paying a premium of roughly $10–14 per 100 GSM. The question is whether the *other* things that
premium buys are worth it to you.

## Where the premium is fair — and where it isn't

**Fair:** You're paying for **density most brands don't offer at all.** A genuine 530 GSM cotton
hoodie is hard to find at any price, and almost nobody publishes the number so you can verify it.
Jamroc does, on 78 of its styles. If a heavy, structured cotton hoodie is specifically what you want,
few brands even compete — the premium buys something real.

**Fair:** The **size range**. Jamroc runs 2XS through 6XL, with 96 styles reaching 3XL or beyond.
Brands that carry both tiny and extended sizes are rare, and that breadth has real value if you sit
at either end.

**Less fair:** The **tees**. At $85–120 for a heavyweight tee, you're paying hoodie money for a
shirt. The fabric is genuinely heavy (310–430 GSM), but the price ceiling is steep — the under-$50
tees are the better-value entry.

**Depends:** The **design.** These are original Jamaican streetwear graphics, not licensed prints.
If that aesthetic is what you're after, it's part of the value. If you just want a heavy blank, you're
paying for design you don't need.
