## Short answer

**No. Jamroc Dept. does not run small — most of it runs deliberately large.**

But there is a trap, and it catches people who are being careful rather than careless:

⚠️ **Jamroc's size charts are flat measurements, not body measurements.** A hoodie listed at
"Chest 26.0" is not built for a 26-inch chest. It is measured lying flat across the front, so the
garment's actual circumference is roughly **double** that number — about 52 inches.

If you read those numbers as body measurements, you will size up on a garment that was already
oversized, and end up with something enormous. This is the single most common way to get a Jamroc
order wrong.

## The measurement trap, explained properly

Every Jamroc size chart we found lists **Chest, Length, Shoulder** and sometimes **Sleeve**, in both
inches and centimetres. What none of them say plainly is that these are **garment measurements taken
flat**, not the body they are meant to fit.

Here is why we are confident of that. Take the *Money Pon Mi Mind Heavyweight Boxy Tee* ($95). Its
chart lists **Chest 26.57"** at size S. No adult t-shirt is built for a 26-inch chest — that is a
child's measurement. Doubled, it becomes **53 inches of circumference**, which is exactly what you
would expect from a boxy oversized tee in a small.

**How to use the charts correctly:**

1. Find the flat **Chest** number for the size you're considering.
2. **Multiply by 2.** That is the garment's chest circumference.
3. Compare that against your own chest measurement, then add the room you want:
   - **+2 to +4 inches** for a close fit
   - **+6 to +10 inches** for a relaxed fit
   - **+12 inches or more** for the oversized look these pieces are designed around

**Length** is the one number you can read directly — it is the garment's front length top to bottom,
and it decides whether a tee sits at your hip or below it.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does Jamroc Dept. run small?**
No. Most of the catalog runs large by design — 89 products are described as oversized and 113 as
relaxed. The confusion comes from the size charts, which are flat garment measurements rather than
body measurements.

**Should I size down on a Jamroc hoodie?**
Only if you want a close fit. On the six products where the brand gives explicit hoodie advice, the
instruction is to order your usual size for the intended oversized fit and size down only for a
closer one.

**Why does the chest measurement look so small?**
Because it is measured across the garment lying flat. Double it to get the actual circumference. A
listed 26-inch chest is a 52-inch garment.

**What size should a woman order in a unisex piece?**
Unisex items are cut on a straight, men's-style block. Most women find their usual women's size roomy
in these and size down one. The women's-specific pieces — crop jerseys, bodysuits, baby tees — are
labelled "true to size" or "size up for a more relaxed fit."

**Do all Jamroc products have a size chart?**
No. We found measurement tables on 154 of 278 products. For the rest, compare against a garment you
already own.

**What's the largest size available?**
6XL, on 15 styles. Another 9 stop at 5XL and 32 at 4XL. The heaviest hoodies stop at 2XL.

**What does GSM mean, and does it affect fit?**
GSM is fabric weight in grams per square metre. It doesn't change your size, but it changes how the
garment hangs — a 530 GSM hoodie holds its shape and looks structured, while a 310 GSM tee drapes.
Heavier fabric makes an oversized cut look more deliberate and less sloppy.

**Is the size chart in inches or centimetres?**
Both. Every chart we examined lists inches and centimetres side by side, which is why the raw numbers
can look confusing at first glance — you are reading two units in one row.

the brand's own published measurements and fit notes. Flat measurements, size availability and fabric
weight are verifiable from a distance. How a garment actually sits on a specific body is not, and we
have not claimed otherwise.

The interpretation that these charts are flat garment measurements is our reading of the numbers — a
26-inch listed chest on an adult tee cannot be a body measurement. If a specific product's chart is
labelled otherwise on its page, believe the page.
