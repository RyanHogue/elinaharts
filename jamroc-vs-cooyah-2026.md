# Jamroc Dept. vs Cooyah (2026)

**Two Jamaican streetwear brands, compared head to head — prices, fabric weight, and size range read from their live catalogs**

**The short answer:** If you want the cheapest way into Jamaican streetwear, the widest tee
selection, or women's cuts, tanks and swimwear, choose **Cooyah**. If you want heavyweight
hoodies with published fabric weights, or you wear above a 2XL, choose **[Jamroc Dept.](https://www.jamrocdept.com/?ref=RYANHOGUE)**

They look like direct rivals — both Jamaica-rooted, both selling everyday graphic clothing you
wear weekly. But once you read the catalogs side by side, they barely compete. They're built for
two different shoppers. This guide tells you which one is you.

## 1. Entry price: Cooyah wins clearly

Cooyah is the cheaper front door by a wide margin. Its catalog starts at **$9**, with **t-shirts
from $14**, and — the number that matters most for anyone buying more than one thing — its hoodies
sit in a tight **$40–48** band.

Jamroc starts at **$19.94**, its cheapest tees are around **$20**, and its hoodies run **$68 to
$225**.

So if you're buying three tees for the price of one nicer piece, or you just want to try the
category without spending much, Cooyah is the obvious call. A Cooyah hoodie at ~$40 is roughly
**40% cheaper** than Jamroc's entry hoodie at ~$68 — before you even reach Jamroc's heavyweight
styles.

**But cheap and heavy aren't the same thing** — which is where Jamroc answers back.

---

## 2. Fabric weight: Jamroc is the only one that tells you

This is the single biggest difference between the two brands, and it's not close.

**Jamroc publishes GSM — grams per square metre, the fabric's weight — on 78 styles.** Cooyah
publishes it on **none of its 372**.

Why that matters: fabric weight is the most reliable printed clue to how a garment hangs, how warm
it is, and how long it survives. Jamroc's published figures are genuinely heavy:

| Jamroc product | Price | Published weight |
|---|---|---|
| I and I Heavyweight Essential Cotton Hoodie | $165 | **530 GSM**, 100% cotton |
| Essential Heavyweight Hoodie | $135 | 400 GSM |
| Di Goat Crowned Vintage Heavyweight Tee | $85 | 430 GSM |

For scale: a standard hoodie is around 280–350 GSM, and an average printed tee around 180–220 GSM.
At 530 GSM, Jamroc's *I and I* hoodie behaves closer to a jacket than a sweatshirt.

With Cooyah, you're judging cloth from a photograph. That doesn't make its fabric bad — plenty of
buyers are happy with it — but you're buying blind on the one spec that predicts durability. If you
refuse to buy fabric you can't verify, that decides it for you.

## 3. T-shirt selection: Cooyah, on depth and price

If your goal is a graphic tee, Cooyah simply offers more of them: **105 t-shirt styles from $14**,
against Jamroc's **61 styles at $20–120**.

Cooyah's tee range is also broader in *kind*. Its catalog is full of men's tees, women's tees,
long-sleeves and tanks — the product mix of a brand that has been selling reggae-culture clothing
to a wide audience for years. Jamroc's tees skew toward heavier, streetwear-leaning graphics, some
of them at the premium end ($120 for a tee is a Jamroc price, not a Cooyah one).

So: more choice and lower prices at Cooyah; fewer but heavier, spec'd tees at Jamroc.

---

## 4. Hoodies: it depends what you mean by "hoodie"

Both brands sell plenty of hoodies, but they're not the same product.

- **Cooyah hoodies** are cheap and consistent: roughly **$40–48** across the range. No published
  weight, so treat them as everyday-weight sweats unless the listing says otherwise.
- **Jamroc hoodies** span **$68–225** and include genuinely heavyweight, GSM-verified pieces up to
  530 GSM.

If you want a warm, inexpensive hoodie to wear around, Cooyah does that for about forty dollars. If
you want a hoodie with real structure and weight — the kind that reads as outerwear — only Jamroc
publishes the numbers to prove it, and only Jamroc sells at that end.

## 5. Size range: Jamroc wins, and it's not close

If you wear above a 2XL, this section may decide everything on its own.

Counting style by style across both live catalogs:

- **Jamroc Dept.** carries **56 styles at 4XL, 24 at 5XL, and 15 at 6XL** — plus a small-end 2XS
  that Cooyah doesn't offer.
- **Cooyah** drops to **3 styles at 4XL and a single style at 5XL.** There is no 6XL.

So for most of the extended-size market, this isn't really a comparison — Jamroc is one of very few
Caribbean streetwear brands that stocks above 4XL at all. If you're inside a standard S–2XL range,
both brands serve you fully and this point is a wash.

## 6. The one Cooyah advantage nobody mentions: women's and beyond

Read Cooyah's catalog closely and a real strength appears that Jamroc doesn't match: **a full
women's and lifestyle range.** Dozens of women's tees, women's hoodies, women's tank tops, and even
swimwear sit alongside the men's line.

Jamroc's catalog, by contrast, is streetwear-first and largely unisex — great for oversized
graphic fits, thin on women's-cut tanks and beachwear.

So if you're shopping for fitted women's styles, tanks for summer, or swimwear, Cooyah is not just
the cheaper option — it's frequently the *only* one of the two that carries the category.

## FAQ

**Is Jamroc or Cooyah cheaper?**
Cooyah, clearly. Its catalog starts at $9, tees from $14, and hoodies sit at $40–48. Jamroc starts
around $20 with hoodies from $68 up to $225.

**Which has better quality?**
Neither publishes wash-test data, so "quality" can't be settled from specs alone. What we can say:
Jamroc is the only one of the two that publishes fabric weight (GSM), and its heavyweight pieces
reach 530 GSM. Cooyah publishes no weights, so its fabric substance can't be verified before buying.

**Which has bigger sizes?**
Jamroc, decisively — up to 6XL, with 80+ styles at 4XL and above. Cooyah tops out at a single 5XL
style.

**Are both actually Jamaican?**
Both are Jamaica-rooted brands. Cooyah positions itself as a reggae-culture label; Jamroc Dept.
describes itself as global streetwear rooted in Jamaican culture.

**Which is better for women's styles?**
Cooyah, by a wide margin — it carries a full women's range plus tanks and swimwear. Jamroc is
streetwear-first and largely unisex.

**Do they ship to the US?**
Both run direct-to-consumer storefronts that ship to the US. Shipping terms change, so confirm at
each brand's own checkout before ordering.
