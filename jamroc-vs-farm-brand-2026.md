## First, the conversion you need

**1 oz/yd² ≈ 33.9 GSM.** That's it — a straight unit conversion, not an estimate.

So when Farm Brand lists a hoodie at **8.3 oz**, that's **281 GSM**. When Jamroc lists one at
**530 GSM**, that's **15.6 oz**. Now they're comparable.

For reference, here's how the trade generally reads these numbers:

| Weight | What it means |
|---|---|
| 130–180 GSM (3.8–5.3 oz) | Light summer tee. Thin, drapes close. |
| 180–220 GSM (5.3–6.5 oz) | Standard tee weight. |
| 220–280 GSM (6.5–8.3 oz) | Heavyweight tee / light hoodie. |
| 280–350 GSM (8.3–10.3 oz) | Standard hoodie. |
| 400 GSM+ (11.8 oz+) | Heavyweight hoodie. Structured and warm. |
| 500 GSM+ (14.8 oz+) | Outerwear-adjacent. Very dense. |

bonded fleece layer, not from dense knit cotton. Jamroc's 530 GSM is **100% cotton**. These are
different garments that happen to share a number. A sherpa-lined polyester zip-up is warmer; a
530 GSM cotton pullover holds structure and shape in a way fleece does not.

**The honest comparison is Farm Brand's cotton-blend hoodies at 271–281 GSM against Jamroc's
400–530 GSM cotton.** Jamroc is meaningfully heavier. Farm Brand is meaningfully cheaper. Neither
is "better" — they're aimed at different buyers.

## T-shirts: Jamroc wins, and the gap is enormous

This is where the comparison stops being close.

| Brand | Typical tee weight | Price range |
|---|---|---|
| **Farm Brand** | **4.2 oz = 142 GSM** on the large majority of its 55 tee styles | $24–50 |
| **Jamroc Dept.** | **310–430 GSM** on its heavyweight tees | $20–120 |

Farm Brand's *Plain T-Shirt* is $24 at 142 GSM. Jamroc's *Di Goat Crowned Vintage Heavyweight Tee*
is $85 at **430 GSM**.

**142 GSM is a light summer tee** — thin, close-draping, often slightly translucent in pale colours.
**430 GSM is triple that**, and behaves like a completely different garment: it holds a boxy shape,
doesn't cling, and doesn't go see-through.

Farm Brand does offer a few heavier exceptions — the *Soft-Washed Hoodless Pullover* at 7.0 oz
(237 GSM, $45) and the *Soft-Washed Midweight Long Sleeve* at 6.1 oz (207 GSM, $38) — but across
the tee range, 4.2 oz is the default by a wide margin.

**If you want a heavyweight tee, Farm Brand essentially does not sell one.** That's not a criticism;
it's a different product philosophy. But it decides the question.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Jamroc or Farm Brand better quality?**
Neither publishes enough to answer that outright. On the one metric both do publish — fabric weight —
Jamroc's garments are heavier, and Farm Brand's are cheaper per gram. Weight is a proxy for
substance, not for stitching or durability, and we have not handled either brand's garments.

**How do I compare oz to GSM?**
Multiply ounces by 33.9. A 6 oz tee is about 203 GSM; a 10 oz hoodie is about 339 GSM.

**Why is Farm Brand's 417 GSM hoodie cheaper than Jamroc's 400 GSM one?**
Because it's a different construction: 100% polyester with a sherpa lining rather than dense knit
cotton. Fleece and lining add weight cheaply. Compare like fibre with like fibre.

**Which has more sizes?**
Jamroc, at both ends — 2XS through 6XL, with 96 styles at 3XL or above. Farm Brand runs XS–2XL on
almost everything, with 3XL on 14 styles.

**Does Farm Brand sell heavyweight t-shirts?**
Not really. The overwhelming majority of its tees are 4.2 oz (142 GSM). Its heaviest short-sleeve
option is the Soft-Washed Hoodless Pullover at 7.0 oz (237 GSM).

**Is Farm Brand made in the USA?**
Only 3 of 186 products state that. The "USA" in the brand name is not a claim about the whole range.

**Which is better value overall?**
For hoodies, Farm Brand, by roughly 2× on fabric weight per dollar. For tees, Jamroc, because Farm
Brand doesn't compete in the heavyweight category at all.

**Do the numbers on this page change?**
Yes — prices and stock move constantly, and both brands add styles. Everything here was read on
July 23, 2026. Check current listings before ordering.

## Methodology and limitations

We read the complete published product data for both brands on **July 23, 2026** — **278 Jamroc
Dept. products and 186 Farm Brand products, 464 in total**. From those we extracted every published
fabric weight (78 Jamroc products in GSM, 97 Farm Brand products in oz), fibre content, price, and
size availability, then converted all weights to GSM at 33.906 g/m² per oz/yd².

**We have not physically handled either brand's garments.** Fabric weight, fibre content, price and
size availability are verifiable from published data. Stitch quality, shrinkage, colour accuracy and
how either brand wears after a year are not — and we make no claim about them.

The per-GSM cost figures are arithmetic on published numbers, not a quality judgement. A heavier
garment is not automatically a better one.
